## The headline finding

The original CEO-082 diagnosis identified three causes of the 2-second store click lag:

1. **NO HTML CACHING** — described as ~60% of the lag. Brief recommended `export const revalidate = 300` on the homepage, /browse, /collections, /book/[slug].
2. **Cover images served at 3840w for 180px slots** — described as the second-biggest lever.
3. **Homepage rendering same book 3x** across independent carousels.

Earlier sessions (2026-04-25 05:46–07:52 UTC, four commits) shipped fixes for #2, #3, and the secondary issues (font self-hosting, priority hint). Those ARE live and effective. They also shipped `revalidate = 300` on /browse and /collections (the cause-#1 fix).

This session discovered: **the `revalidate = 300` exports are no-ops in production**. `lib/supabase/server.ts createClient()` reads `cookies()` from `next/headers`. Per Next.js App Router rules, any page that calls a function reading `cookies()` is forced to render dynamically regardless of `revalidate`. Production headers confirm: `cache-control: private, no-cache, no-store, max-age=0` and `x-vercel-cache: MISS` on /browse even after the new deploy.

So the biggest single lever (cause-#1, ~60% of lag) is **not actually fixed**. The other 40% IS fixed. Net: the store should be meaningfully faster on cover-image-heavy interactions and on repeat visits (storage cacheControl now 1 year, immutable), but first-paint render time on /browse and /collections is unchanged because each request still does a full SSR.

CEO-095 is the actual fix and it's well-scoped. Recommendation: do CEO-095 next, then CEO-096 measurement, in that order.
