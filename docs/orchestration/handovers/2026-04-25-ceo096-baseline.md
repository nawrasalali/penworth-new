## What is NOT closed by this work

The Lighthouse Performance scores are still in the 57-67 band ("needs improvement" tier). Two issues drive that, both **independent of the 2s-lag thread**:

1. **TBT (Total Blocking Time) 2.2-6.2s across all pages.** Means the client bundle is doing meaningful main-thread work after hydration. Not a server-side latency issue. Worth a future ticket; not part of CEO-082's diagnosis.

2. **LCP 2.8-4.1s.** Marginally over Google's 2.5s "good" cutoff. The cover image is the LCP element on most surfaces; even with the cover-image weight cap from CEO-082, the network priority/lazy-loading interaction can push it past 2.5s on a slow mobile profile. Tightening (`priority={true}` on the hero card, narrower `sizes` prop) was in CEO-082's "explicitly not shipped" list.

Neither of these blocks closing the 2s-lag thread, because the original goal was server-side latency, not client-side performance score. Recommend spawning a separate performance ticket for client-side TBT and LCP if/when launch traffic warrants it. Not blocking; not on the critical path.

* **Cold vs warm Lighthouse semantics:** Lighthouse navigates with a fresh browser context per run, but it does NOT bypass server-side caching. Whether you get PRERENDER or HIT depends on whether anyone primed your CDN region recently. The two homepage runs in this baseline (2421ms vs 83ms TTFB) demonstrate this directly; both are valid post-fix numbers, they just sample different cache states.
